Discover Ethiopia - History,culture and leaders

The Ethiopian Hidase Dam, also known as the Grand Ethiopian Renaissance Dam (GERD), is one of Africa's largest hydroelectric dams. Built on the Blue Nile River, it represents Ethiopia's commitment to energy independence and economic development. The dam will generate electricity for millions of Ethiopians and support the country's growing economy.

About Ethiopia

Where is ethiopia?

Ethiopia is in the Horn of Africa. It is a highland country with mountains, lakes, rivers and many different climates.

People and language

Ethiopia has more than 80 ethnic groups and many languages, including Amharic, Afaan Oromo, Tigrinya and Somali.

Ethiopian calendar

Ethiopia uses its own calendar with 13 months, and many people use a local time system in everyday life.

Ethiopia and independence

Ethiopa Never Colonised

Ethiopia was never fully colonised by a European power. There was an Italian occupation between 1936 and 1941, but Ethiopians kept their national identity and independence.

Battle of Adwa

In 1896, Ethiopian forces led by Emperor Menelik II defeated the Italian army at the Battle of Adwa. This victory inspired many Africans in their struggle against colonialism.

Addis Ababa

Addis Ababa is sometimes called the "capital of Africa" because it hosts the African Union and many international organisations.

Ethiopian Leaders

Emperor Tewedross II

Tewodros II tried to unify and modernise Ethiopia in the 19th century. He is remembered as a strong leader who wanted a united country.

Emperor Minilik II

Menelik II led Ethiopia to victory at the Battle of Adwa in 1896, stopping Italian colonisation and protecting Ethiopia's independence.

Emperor Haile Selassie I

Haile Selassie ruled for many years and helped to found the Organisation of African Unity, giving Ethiopia a big role in African politics.

Mengistu Hailemariam

Mengistu Hailemariam led Ethiopia after the monarchy ended. His government was known as the Derg, a military regime.

Meles Zenawi

Meles Zenawi was prime minister for many years and helped rebuild the country after civil war, focusing on development and infrastructure.

Hailemariam Desalegn

Hailemariam Desalegn became prime minister after Meles Zenawi and led the country during a period of rapid growth.

Abiy Ahmed

Abiy Ahmed is a recent prime minister who has led Ethiopia in the 21st century and received the Nobel Peace Prize in 2019.

Festivals and Celebrations

Five example festivals, each with its own image and explanation.

Meskel

Meskel (Finding of the True Cross) is celebrated with a large bonfire called demera, songs and traditional clothes.

Timkat

Timkat (Epiphany) is a baptism celebration with priests carrying tabots, music and water blessings.

Irreecha

Irreecha is an Oromo thanksgiving festival held near lakes and rivers, giving thanks to God for peace and harvest.

Adwa Commemoration

Every year people remember the Battle of Adwa with marches, speeches and music to honour the victory over colonial forces.

Eid Al-Adha

Eid Al-Adha is an important Muslim celebration in Ethiopia, where people gather for prayers, share meals and celebrate with family and friends.

Regions and Landscapes

From the highlands of Amhara and Tigray to the lowlands of Afar and Somali, Ethiopia has mountains, deserts, rift valley lakes and green forests. Each region has its own music, food and traditional clothing.

Kampata

Kampata is one of Ethiopia's beautiful regions with diverse landscapes and cultural heritage.

Afar Dallol

Afar Dallol is one of the hottest places on Earth, known for its colorful salt formations and volcanic landscapes.

Abay River

The Abay River (Blue Nile) flows through Ethiopia, providing water and supporting agriculture and communities.
